Encryption

May 10, 2022 Roel van der Meer

Encryption is the method of encoding information. In encryption, plain text – which is the actual representation of the information – is passed through an algorithm to produce a ciphertext – which is an alternative form of the actual information. Encryption ensures that only authorized parties can decipher the ciphertext to recreate the plain text. The process of encrypting and decrypting information is known as cryptography. Encryption uses an algorithm or a cipher which includes a variable. This variable (Key) is what makes the cipher unique. A combination of knowing the algorithm used and the key used as a variable is what makes encryption such a valuable security tool.

Encryption has long been implemented by the military for secure communication. It is also being used to secure data in transit on a network. There are two types of encryption. Symmetric encryption and asymmetric encryption.

In symmetric encryption, the encryption and decryption keys are the same. This key is shared between communicating parties. The weak point of this encryption is the transfer of keys between the parties involved. This encryption is best used for one-to-one sharing.

Asymmetric encryption (public key encryption), is a type of encryption where the encryption key is published and can be viewed by everyone but the decryption key is private and can only be accessed by the recipient.
