Frequency Division Duplex

May 10, 2022 Roel van der Meer

Frequency Division Duplex (FDD) is a duplex communication technique in which connected parties transmit at different frequencies for upstream and downstream communication. Because the data is transmitted at different frequency bands, transmission and reception can be achieved at the same time and the data being sent and received do not interfere with each other. In FDD, to avoid interference between transmitting and receiving signals, it is necessary to provide sufficient gap between communicating frequencies, this is known as a guard band. FDD is inefficient if the transmission pattern is unequal because bandwidth is allocated by regulators and capacity in both directions can only be shaped by reallocating channels.

FDD is widely implemented in mobile communication. In mobile communication, one block of electromagnetic spectrum is allocated to the uplink – between the mobile user and the base station while a separate block of electromagnetic spectrum is allocated for the downlink – communication from the base station back to the mobile user. Another implementation of FDD is in Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Line (ADSL) and Very high bit rate Digital Subscriber Line (VDSL) for data services, FDD is also implemented in UTMS/WCDMA frequency division duplexing mode as well as the CDMA2000 system. IEEE 802.16 defines another implementation of FDD in WiMax frequency division duplexing mode.
